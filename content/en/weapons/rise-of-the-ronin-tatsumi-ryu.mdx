<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding the Rise of the Ronin Tatsumi Ryu Style">

The **rise of the ronin tatsumi ryu** combat style represents the pinnacle of speed and precision for Katana users in Bakumatsu-era Japan. As a **Jin** (Human) alignment style, it is designed to counter lightweight weapons and fast-attacking opponents. Unlike the more balanced Ten styles or the heavy-hitting Chi stances, Tatsumi-ryu focuses on Iaijutsu—the art of drawing the sword, striking, and re-sheathing in one fluid motion.

In the 2026 meta, this style remains a favorite for "Midnight" difficulty players because its Counterspark (parry) has minimal startup frames. This allows you to react to the rapid-fire combinations of late-game bosses like those found in the Shinsengumi or Western factions.

| Feature | Specification | Combat Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon Type** | Katana | Most versatile primary weapon |
| **Style Alignment** | **Jin (Human)** | Strong vs. Sabers/Lightweight |
| **Attack Speed** | Very High | Excellent for pressure |
| **Ki Consumption** | Moderate | Requires Blade Flash management |
| **Counterspark** | Instant Draw | Best for fast multi-hits |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Tatsumi-ryu excels at "Panic" management. Because the attacks are so fast, you can often trigger a Ki Break on an enemy before they can recover from a successful Counterspark.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Unlock and Master Tatsumi Ryu">

Unlocking the **rise of the ronin tatsumi ryu** style requires navigating the political landscape of the Shogunate. This style is intrinsically tied to **Naosuke Ii**, the Chief Minister of the Shogunate. Depending on your story choices, the method of acquisition may vary, but it generally follows a path of bond-building or martial testing.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Naosuke Ii">
    Progress through the main story in Yokohama until you reach the Edo region. Naosuke Ii is a central figure in the Pro-Shogunate missions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish a Bond">
    Interact with Naosuke Ii through story dialogues and gift-giving. Focus on items that appeal to his refined tastes to raise your Bond Level quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Complete Bond Missions">
    Successfully finish missions associated with the Shogunate's internal affairs. These missions often reward you with the initial "Novice" rank of the Tatsumi-ryu style.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Dojo Training">
    To reach Master rank, visit the Dojo in Edo or Yokohama and select Tatsumi-ryu as your training focus. Achieving a "Master" rating in combat trials is required to unlock the final Martial Skills.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Missable Content">
If your story choices lead to a hostile relationship with the Shogunate early on, you may need to use the **Testament of the Soul** at your Longhouse to revisit earlier missions and repair the bond with Naosuke Ii to unlock this style.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Strategy and Matchup Effectiveness">

The effectiveness of **rise of the ronin tatsumi ryu** depends heavily on the "Rock-Paper-Scissors" combat style system. As a Jin style, you must be mindful of the enemy's weapon type. Using Tatsumi-ryu against a heavy Odachi user (Chi-type) will put you at a disadvantage, reducing the Ki damage you deal and increasing the Ki you lose when blocked.

| Enemy Weapon Category | Style Advantage | Recommended Tactic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sabers / Rapiers** | **High Advantage** | Aggressive Countersparking |
| **Bayonets** | Advantage | Close the gap quickly |
| **Katanas** | Neutral | Focus on Martial Skill punishes |
| **Odachi / Greatswords** | **Disadvantage** | Switch to Chi-style Katana |
| **Spears** | Neutral | Use reach-closing skills |

<Callout type="success" title="Tactical Advantage">
When the blue "Up" arrow appears next to the enemy's health bar, your Tatsumi-ryu style is in an advantageous matchup. During these windows, your Countersparks will stagger the enemy much longer, allowing for a guaranteed Critical Hit if their Ki is low.
</Callout>

</Section>
