<Section icon={Shield} title="Mastering Style Matchups: Ten, Chi, and Jin">

In Rise of the Ronin, combat is a game of "Rock-Paper-Scissors" defined by your style matchups. For the Oxtail Blade, switching between styles is not just optional—it is a requirement for survival in high-level duels. Each style provides a different Counterspark animation, which is the core defensive and offensive mechanic of the game.

The three primary style categories for the Oxtail Blade interact with enemy weapons as follows:

| Style Category | Effective Against | Weak Against | Counterspark Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ten (Sky)** | Katanas, Medium Weapons | Sabers, Light Weapons | Traditional parry with a vertical follow-up |
| **Chi (Earth)** | Odachis, Heavy Weapons | Katanas, Medium Weapons | Heavy deflection that creates massive Ki stagger |
| **Jin (Human)** | Sabers, Light Weapons | Odachis, Heavy Weapons | High-speed spin that parries multi-hit strings |
| **Shinobi** | Specialist / All | Standard Matchups | Precise, high-risk parry with lethal follow-ups |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Always watch the blue or red indicator next to the enemy's health bar. If you see a red down-arrow, your current style is disadvantaged. Switch immediately to the advantageous style to increase your Ki damage and stagger potential.
</Callout>

Using the correct **rise of the ronin oxtail blade styles** ensures that your Countersparks cause the "Panic" status more quickly. When an enemy is panicked, their Ki recovery stops, and your Martial Skills deal significantly more damage. This is the primary loop for defeating bosses in the Yokohama and Edo regions.

Progression in Rise of the Ronin is tied deeply to the Bond system. You cannot simply find every style in a treasure chest; you must earn them by building relationships with key historical figures or completing specific missions.
