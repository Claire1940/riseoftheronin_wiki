<Section icon={Shield} title="Blacksmithing: Upgrading vs. Bond Transfers">

A common mistake is focusing solely on player level. In reality, your **Equipment Level** dictates your performance. A level 40 Ronin wearing level 20 gear will struggle significantly against level 35 enemies. The Blacksmith is your most important ally in keeping your gear relevant without losing your favorite set bonuses.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Disassemble Rare Gear">
    Never sell high-rarity (Yellow/Orange) gear unless you are desperate for cash. Disassembling provides rare materials required for high-level upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Vendor Levels">
    Vendors always sell gear at or near your current maximum equipment level. If you need a quick power boost, buying a basic weapon from a high-level region vendor is often cheaper than upgrading an old one.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Perform Bond Transfers">
    Identify weapons with empty passive slots. Use **Bond Jewels** to move powerful traits (like +% Attack or Reduced Ki Consumption) from your old, low-level gear to your new high-level base.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade Core Stats">
    Use the "Upgrade" menu to increase the base attack of your weapon. Note that this only increases raw numbers, not the percentage-based passive stats.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip: Treasure Hunting">
Returning to early-game areas to open missed treasure chests is highly rewarding. The loot inside scales to your current level, providing an easy way to get high-level gear from weak enemies.
</Callout>

</Section>
