<Section icon={Swords} title="Melee Weapons Tier List and Selection">

To master **rise of the ronin gameplay**, players must understand how weapons and combat styles interact. The game features nine distinct melee weapon categories, along with an unarmed combat option. Each weapon offers a different balance of speed, reach, damage, and Ki pressure. Ki pressure is the ability to deplete an opponent's stamina gauge, leaving them vulnerable to devastating Critical Hits. 

While every weapon is viable when paired with the correct combat style, some options provide more consistency during difficult boss encounters and general open-world exploration. Our tier list ranks these weapons based on ease of use, attack recovery speed, and versatility in duels.

| Weapon | Tier | Damage | Speed | Reach | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Katana** | S | Medium-High | High | Medium | Balanced dueling and Counterspark learning |
| **Paired Swords** | S | Medium | Very High | Short | High pressure and rapid style switching |
| **Oxtail Blade** | S | Medium-High | Very High | Long | High mobility and crowd control |
| **Polearm** | A | High | Medium | Very Long | Safe spacing and group fights |
| **Odachi** | A | Very High | Medium-Low | Long | Stance breaking and heavy punish damage |
| **Spear** | A | Medium-High | Medium | Long | Defensive spacing and thrust attacks |
| **Saber** | A | Medium | High | Medium | Mobile dueling and quick counter-attacks |
| **Bayonet** | A | Medium | Medium | Long | Hybrid melee and ranged firearm combat |
| **Greatsword** | B | Very High | Low | Long | Maximum single-hit damage and heavy punishes |
| **Unarmed** | C | Low | High | Very Short | Challenge runs and non-lethal combat |

The Katana is the most versatile starting option, featuring the largest selection of combat styles. It allows players to adapt to almost any enemy matchup. For players who prefer aggressive, fast-paced combat, the Paired Swords and Oxtail Blade offer rapid attack recovery, making it easier to cancel animations and perform defensive maneuvers. Heavy weapons like the Odachi and Greatsword deal massive damage but require precise timing, as missed swings leave the player highly vulnerable to counter-attacks.

<Callout type="tip" title="Weapon Selection Tip">
Always equip two primary weapons that complement each other. Pairing a fast, short-range weapon like the Paired Swords with a long-reaching option like the Polearm ensures you can handle both swift single targets and large groups of enemies.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Styles and Counterspark Mechanics">

Combat styles determine your weapon's moveset, special Martial Skills, and parry animations. The combat system operates on a rock-paper-scissors dynamic split across three primary stances: Ten (Sky), Chi (Earth), and Jin (Human). There are also specialized Shinobi styles that offer unique, stealth-oriented movements. 

Matching your style against the enemy's weapon is crucial. An advantageous matchup increases the Ki damage you inflict and enhances the stagger effect of your Countersparks.

| Style | Meaning | Strong Against | Weak Against | Recommended Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ten (Sky)** | Sky | Katanas and medium weapons | Sabers and light weapons | Traditional sword duels and standard enemies |
| **Chi (Earth)** | Earth | Odachis and heavy weapons | Katanas and medium weapons | Interrupting slow, heavy attacks |
| **Jin (Human)** | Human | Sabers and light weapons | Odachis and heavy weapons | Countering fast Western blades and rapid strikes |
| **Shinobi** | Specialist | None (Disadvantaged) | Standard weapons | High-risk, high-reward precise Countersparks |

The Counterspark is both an offensive strike and a parry. Activating a Counterspark just before an enemy's attack lands will deflect the blow, severely damaging their Ki and temporarily putting them into a panicked state. When an enemy is panicked, your attacks deal increased health and Ki damage.

For advanced Ki management, players must use the Blade Flash mechanic. Pressing the stance-switch button immediately after an attack string cleans blood from your weapon, instantly restoring a portion of your consumed Ki. This allows you to maintain sustained offensive pressure without exhausting your stamina.

<Callout type="warning" title="Defensive Caution">
Do not attempt to Counterspark every single attack in a multi-hit combination. If you are unfamiliar with an enemy's attack rhythm, it is much safer to hold the block button and only attempt to Counterspark the final strike of their combo.
</Callout>

</Section>

While you can reset your skill points later at your Longhouse, focusing on a cohesive build early on makes tough encounters much more manageable.

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Beginner Progression Route">

Navigating the transition from the linear prologue to the open world of Yokohama can be challenging. Following a structured progression route ensures you collect essential gear, unlock powerful combat styles, and level up efficiently without hitting artificial difficulty walls.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starting Difficulty">
    Choose between Dawn (Easy), Dusk (Normal), or Twilight (Hard). Dawn is excellent for players who want to focus on exploration and story, as it increases healing item effectiveness and reduces Ki consumption. You can freely change this setting at any time from the game menu.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete the Prologue and Learn Basics">
    Use the linear opening section on the Black Ships to practice defensive mechanics. Focus on learning the timing of the Counterspark against basic soldiers and understand how your primary weapon's attack animations recovery feels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear Hostile Bases in Yokohama">
    Upon reaching the open world, do not rush directly to main story markers. Instead, locate nearby red areas on your map to restore Public Order. Clearing these bandit camps unlocks local merchants, reveals hidden collectibles, and rewards you with high-quality gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Establish and Upgrade Your Longhouse">
    Once you unlock your city lodging (the Longhouse), use it to manage your companions. Talk with visiting allies, give them preferred gifts to raise your bond levels, and customize your character's appearance. This is also where you can reset your skill points if needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Note">
Raising regional bonds is just as important as leveling up your character. High regional bonds grant permanent discounts at local shops and reveal the locations of rare fugitives, who drop some of the best early-game combat styles.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Multiplayer Co-op and World Exploration">

The open world of late-Edo Japan is filled with activities that can be tackled solo or with up to two other players. Cooperative multiplayer is mission-based rather than a seamless shared open world. Players can recruit friends or random allies from the cooperative setup screen before launching major story operations.

| Feature | Solo Gameplay | Online Co-op Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **World Exploration** | Full open-world access across all regions | Restricted to specific mission instances |
| **Ally Selection** | Up to two AI companions or solo play | Up to two online human players |
| **Mission Progress** | Saves directly to host campaign | Progress applies to host; guests earn rewards |
| **Asynchronous Features** | Pilgrim Dogs and wandering ronin active | Disabled during active multiplayer sessions |

While free exploration of the countryside remains a solo experience, the game features asynchronous online elements to keep the world feeling alive. Other players' custom ronin will occasionally appear in your game as wandering NPCs. You can fight them for rare equipment or rescue them from bandits for faction rewards. Additionally, sending out your Pilgrim Dog with a coin deposit allows it to travel to other players' worlds, returning later with rare items and crafting materials.
