<Section icon={Shield} title="Troubleshooting Missing Fugitive Locations">

If you have cleared all visible icons but your regional tracker still shows a **rise of the ronin fugitive missing**, follow these systematic steps to force the spawn or reveal the hidden location. This process ensures that you aren't wasting time wandering through cleared districts.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Check the Collection Menu">
    Open your main menu and navigate to the 'Collection' tab. Select 'Fugitives' to see a list of every named criminal in your current region. Greyed-out names with "???" indicate fugitives you haven't encountered yet.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Advance to Nighttime">
    The most common reason for a missing fugitive is the time requirement. Use the Pocket Watch item to set the time to Midnight. Re-check the map for red skull icons that were previously invisible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Restore Public Order Nearby">
    Some fugitives only spawn as "Reinforcements" or hidden leaders after you have cleared a nearby bandit camp. Ensure every white circle on your map has been turned blue (indicating restored order).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Interact with Local Residents">
    Talk to NPCs with speech bubbles in restored districts. Occasionally, a resident will give you a tip about a "dangerous wanderer," which places a temporary marker on your map for a missing fugitive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Use the Testament of the Soul">
    If you have already moved to Edo or Kyoto, use the Testament of the Soul at your Longhouse. This allows you to return to Yokohama with your current stats and equipment to hunt down lower-level fugitives you missed during the early game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Strategies for Elite Fugitives">

Fugitives are significantly more dangerous than standard enemies. They possess unique Martial Skills and often utilize combat styles that counter the basic "Ten" (Sky) style. To defeat them efficiently, you must master the **Counterspark** timing and manage your **Ki** gauge effectively.

<Table>
| Style Category | Effective Against | Fugitive Type |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Ten (Sky)** | Katanas / Medium Weapons | Standard Ronin / Deserters |
| **Chi (Earth)** | Odachis / Heavy Weapons | Brutes / Axe-wielders |
| **Jin (Human)** | Sabers / Light Weapons | Western Duelists / Assassins |
| **Shinobi** | All (Technical) | Hidden Bosses / Ninja |
| **Ranged** | All (Distance) | Fugitives in towers / Snipers |
</Table>

When engaging a fugitive, focus on damaging their **Maximum Ki**. This is done through successful Countersparks and using Martial Skills when they are "Panicked." Once their Ki is depleted, you can perform a **Critical Hit** for massive damage. If you find a specific fugitive too difficult, check your equipment for "Damage vs. Humans" bonuses.

<Callout type="tip" title="Combat Tip">
Always use a **Blade Flash** (R1 on PS5 / default key on PC) after a successful combo. Cleaning the blood from your blade restores a portion of your Ki, allowing you to maintain pressure on the fugitive without becoming exhausted.
</Callout>
