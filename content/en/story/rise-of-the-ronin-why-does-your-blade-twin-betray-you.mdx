<Section icon={Map} title="The Narrative Origins of the Blade Twin's Betrayal">

In the opening hours of Team NINJA's open-world epic, you are introduced to the Veiled Edge, a pair of highly trained assassins bound by blood and destiny. However, a fateful mission on Commodore Perry’s Black Ships tears this bond apart, leaving many players asking: **rise of the ronin why does your blade twin betray you**? The answer is a complex web of physical trauma, political manipulation, and a divergent ideological path for the future of Bakumatsu-era Japan.

After sacrificing themselves so you can escape the Black Ships, your Blade Twin is captured by the Shogunate's elite protector, the Blue Demon. Instead of being executed, your twin is subjected to intense physical reconstruction—losing an arm and receiving a lethal prosthetic weapon—and systematic ideological brainwashing. When they reappear in Yokohama and Edo, they are no longer the loyal partner you once knew, but a shadowy agent working alongside the Shogunate and foreign powers to enforce their own vision of peace.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Rise of the Ronin Why Does Your Blade Twin Betray You: Faction Choices">

As you navigate the politically fractured landscape of 19th-century Japan, your choices directly influence how you interact with your former partner. The core conflict of the game pits the Pro-Shogunate forces (who wish to maintain the status quo and modernize via foreign treaties) against the Anti-Shogunate faction (who wish to expel foreigners and restore imperial rule). Your alignment determines the context of your clashes and highlights the tragedy of why your blade twin betrays you.

If you choose the Anti-Shogunate path, your twin views you as an agent of chaos who is destabilizing the nation and opening it up to civil war. Conversely, if you align with the Pro-Shogunate forces, your twin still opposes you because they believe the current leadership is too weak to implement the radical changes required for Japan's survival. They have aligned with more extreme elements within both foreign and domestic factions to force modernization through iron-fisted authority.

| Faction Path | Player Allies | Twin's Position | Battle Consequences |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pro-Shogunate | Naosuke Ii, Isami Kondo | Opposes Shogunate weakness | Battles focus on defending key political figures |
| Anti-Shogunate | Ryoma Sakamoto, Kogoro Katsura | Protects Shogunate stability | Battles focus on stopping assassinations and rebellions |

<Callout type="warning" title="Faction Warning">
While faction choices alter dialogue, cutscenes, and available companion missions, they will not prevent the inevitable confrontations with your Blade Twin. Their betrayal is hardcoded into the central narrative, forcing a tragic clash of blades regardless of your political loyalty.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Alter Story Choices with Testament of the Soul">

If you regret the choices that led to your confrontations, or if you want to explore how different decisions affect your twin's dialogue and actions, you do not need to restart your campaign. The developers built a robust mission replay system called the Testament of the Soul, which becomes accessible once you reach the city of Edo.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Longhouse">
    Travel to your active lodging (Longhouse) in Yokohama, Edo, or Kyoto.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Open the Testament of the Soul">
    Interact with the menu inside your Longhouse to view the timeline of completed main and bond missions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select a Key Narrative Node">
    Choose a mission where a major decision was made, such as your sparing or executing Gonzo, or your faction choice at Mibu-dera.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Replay and Change Your Choice">
    Complete the mission under level sync or your current stats, making the opposite choice to update your active story timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="System Requirement">
Using the Testament of the Soul allows you to unlock missed rewards, experience alternate cutscenes, and recruit allies who may have died or departed in your original playthrough. For more details on system compatibility, check the [Rise of the Ronin on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1340990/Rise_of_the_Ronin/) page.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Builds to Counter the Blade Twin">

Your Blade Twin is one of the most agile and punishing bosses in the game. They utilize a custom prosthetic arm that can transition into a grappling hook, a firearm, or a secondary blade. To defeat them, you must optimize your build and understand the combat style matchups. 

The combat system relies on three primary styles: Ten (Sky), Chi (Earth), and Jin (Human). Matching these styles against your twin's active stance is crucial for breaking their Ki and executing Critical Hits.
